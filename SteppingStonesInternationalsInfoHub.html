Stepping Stones Internationals formation.

Stepping Stones International (SSI) is a non-governmental, non-profit organization founded in 2006 in Mochudi, Botswana. Its mission is to unlock the potential of orphaned and vulnerable adolescents aged 12 to 25 through holistic development, strengthening families, and activating sustainable opportunities for youth to become self-sufficient.

Formation Timeline:

2005
  • SSI was registered in the United States under 501(c)(3) status.
2006
   
  • SSI was officially registered in Botswana as a non-profit organization.
  • The organization began its operations by serving 30 orphaned and vulnerable children (OVC) in Mochudi.
    •  
  • It received endorsements from the Ministry of Education and the Department of Social Services as an adolescent OVC program.
  • The Roman Catholic Church donated land in Mochudi for SSI's operations.
2007:
  • SSI initiated a caregiver outreach program.
  •   It established a partnership with the Baylor Teen Club.
    •  
  • Former President Festus Mogae officially opened the new SSI facility in Mochudi.
2008:
  • - Participation increased to 70 participants.
    •  
  • SSI expanded peer education to Kgatleng, North-East, and Good Hope Districts.
    •  
  • The organization launched its first sexual abuse campaign in Mochudi.
2010:
  • SSI received a 3-year funding from the FACET Foundation to fund the Graff Leadership Centre.
    •  
  • It secured a 5-year funding for the Life Skills program from EDC.
    •  
  • The organization was awarded World Bank funding in collaboration with Human People to People to expand community mobilization and peer education in five new districts.
  • SSI developed a training manual for police officers on juvenile justice in collaboration with UNICEF and the Botswana Police Service.

Over the years, SSI has grown significantly, expanding its reach and programs to serve thousands of youth across Botswana. Its comprehensive approach combines life skills, leadership training, psychosocial support, and community mobilization to empower young people to overcome challenges and achieve their full potential.

Stepping Stones Internationals Visions and goals for the future.

Stepping Stones International (SSI) envisions a world where vulnerable youth become empowered leaders who achieve their dreams.

Their goals for the future focus on:

  • Expanding Reach: Building on their success of reaching over 28,000 youth, SSI aims to serve more young people across Botswana and beyond.
  • Enhancing Programs: Continuing to develop holistic programs that combine life skills, leadership, psychosocial support, and community mobilization to empower youth.
  • Strengthening Partnerships: Collaborating with local and international organizations to provide job shadowing experiences and training programs, such as the ABSA Ready to Work initiative.

SSI remains committed to unlocking the potential of vulnerable youth, helping them become self-sufficient and active contributors to their communities.

Stepping Stones International (SSI) offers a comprehensive range of ongoing programs and services aimed at empowering vulnerable youth and their families in Botswana. Here's an overview of their key initiatives:

Psychosocial support & Family Empowerment

Psychosocial Support (PSS)SSI's social workers conduct monthly home visits to provide support, identify issues, and develop family care plans. They also offer referrals to community organizations for additional assistance. Young Mothers Support Group:In-school participants receive education on gender-based violence, equipping them to recognize abuse signs and seek help. DREAMS Botswana Initiative In partnership with PEPFAR and PCI, this program aims to reduce HIV infections among adolescent girls and young women aged 9 to 24. It includes the Pinagare Family Program, which educates caregivers on sexual and reproductive health to support their children effectively. Breaking Barriers to an HIV-Free Generation Supported by USAID, this program focuses on preventing new HIV infections and reducing vulnerability among orphans and vulnerable children (OVCs) and adolescent girls and young women (AGYW) through health services, life skills education, financial literacy, and positive parenting interventions.

Education & literacy programmes

Breakthrough to English Literacy ProgramIn collaboration with the University of Utah Reading Clinic, SSI trains teachers to improve English literacy rates among students. The program has been implemented in 32 schools, enhancing reading fluency and comprehension. STEM Projects Using the Design Squad Global manual, participants engage in engineering and innovation projects, enhancing critical thinking and problem-solving skills with materials available in their communities.

Leadership & Economics

Find the Leader Within This program develops entrepreneurial, career, and leadership skills among youth, boosting their confidence and helping them discover career paths. Many graduates have launched their own businesses. ABSA Ready to Work In partnership with ABSA Botswana, this program provides training in money skills, people skills, work skills, and entrepreneurial skills. Participants receive certificates upon completion, preparing them for the workforce.

Community mobilization & advocacy

Child Protection AdvocacySSI aligns its staff policies with the Botswana Childrens Act of 2009 and advocates for its full implementation. They support child victims of abuse by ensuring access to essential services, including health, psychosocial support, justice advocacy, and education.

SASA! Training

Through the Grannies Tea Group, SSI provides community mobilization training to grandmothers, empowering them to identify and address gender-based violence in their communities.

Community Outreach Initiatives

  •   Stomp Out Sexual Abuse Campaign 
  •    Gender equality workshops 
    •  
  •   Youth career fairs 
    •    
  • Caregiver & peer educator training
SSI leads campaigns like the Stomp Out Sexual Abuse Campaign, conducts workshops on gender equality, and organizes youth career fairs. They also train caregivers and peer educators to address issues like sexual abuse, substance abuse, and intergenerational sex.

Digital engagement

Youth Spot Mobile ApplicationIn partnership with UNICEF, USAID, and Botswana-Baylor Childrens Clinical Centre of Excellence, SSI launched the 'Youth Spot' app. This platform allows youth to access articles, monitor mental health, participate in social chats, and reflect on their lifestyle, contributing to SDG goals related to health, education, and innovation. These programs collectively aim to unlock the potential of vulnerable youth, equipping them with the necessary skills, knowledge, and support to lead healthy, fulfilling lives and contribute positively to their communities.

Stepping Stones Internationals impact to its communities

Stepping Stones International (SSI) has made a powerful impact on communities in Botswana by empowering thousands of vulnerable youth with life skills, leadership training, education support, and psychosocial services. Through its holistic approach, SSI has helped young people stay in school, avoid risky behaviors, start businesses, and become confident leaders in their communities. Families have also been strengthened through caregiver support and community education, leading to safer, more resilient households. SSIs work has not only changed individual lives it has uplifted entire communities by unlocking the potential of the next generation.
